## Part 6: Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### Major Risks

#### 1. Regulatory Risk (HIGH → MEDIUM)
**Risk:** FDA/EU MDR classifies as medical device requiring approval

**Mitigation:**
- Position as "educational tool" not diagnostic device
- Disclaimer: "For learning purposes only, consult a doctor"
- Precedent: Osmosis, WebMD, Ada Health operate without FDA approval
- Stay in wellness/education category, avoid clinical decision support

**Fallback:**
- If forced to pursue FDA approval, pivot to B2B hospital market
- Deep-pocketed institutional customers can afford 3-5 year wait
- Emerging markets (India, Malaysia) have less stringent regulation

---

#### 2. Accuracy & Liability Risk (HIGH → LOW)
**Risk:** AI misdiagnosis leads to harm, lawsuits

**Mitigation:**
- **Terms of Service:** "Educational purposes only, not a substitute for medical advice"
- Insurance: Errors & Omissions policy ($1-2M coverage, $5K/year)
- Validation: Publish accuracy metrics transparently (85-95% sensitivity/specificity)
- Human-in-loop: Always recommend "consult a doctor if concerned"

**Comparison:**
- WebMD has billion-dollar valuation despite lawsuit risks
- Symptom checkers (Ada, Buoy) have $0 liability incidents to date
- Educational positioning = stronger legal defense

---

#### 3. LLM API Cost Risk (MEDIUM → LOW)
**Risk:** OpenAI/Anthropic raises prices, destroys margins

**Mitigation:**
- **Multi-provider architecture:** Support OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Azure
- **Local models backup:** Llama 3, Mistral as fallback
- **Hybrid approach:** 70% local CV, only 30% use LLM
- **Cost caps:** Free tier = local only, paid tier = LLM access

**Math:**
- Current LLM cost: $0.01-0.05 per analysis
- Even if 10x increase → $0.10-0.50 still profitable at $10-50/month pricing
- Price elasticity: Can raise prices $5-10 if needed

---

#### 4. Competition Risk (MEDIUM → LOW)
**Risk:** Google Health, Microsoft Nuance, or incumbents copy your model

**Mitigation:**
- **Speed:** Launch before big tech notices (6-9 months)
- **Network effects:** Medical student community = moat
- **Localization:** Hindi/regional languages = barrier for Western companies
- **Regulatory:** Big tech faces higher scrutiny, slower approvals

**Advantages you have:**
- Bootstrapped = faster decision-making (vs corporate bureaucracy)
- Emerging markets = too small for Google/Microsoft to care (initially)
- 9 models already trained = 18-month head start

---

#### 5. Model Loading/Technical Risk (HIGH → MEDIUM)
**Risk:** Can't load existing models (Keras version incompatibility)

**Mitigation:**
- **Short-term:** Use GPT-4V entirely (pay $0.10-0.50 per analysis)
- **Medium-term:** Re-export models to ONNX format (1-2 weeks work)
- **Long-term:** Re-train in current TensorFlow version (2-3 months)
- **Alternative:** Pivot to 100% LLM (expensive but viable)

**Impact:**
- If forced to use 100% GPT-4V: Increase prices to $20-50/month
- Still competitive vs $200/year education platforms
- Validates product-market fit while fixing technical debt
